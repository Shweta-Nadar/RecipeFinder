Roasted Vegetables

Colorful roasted vegetables with herbs and olive oil

Home Categories Popular Recipes About
Prep Time: 15 mins
Cook Time: 35 mins
Total Time: 50 mins
Servings: 6
Healthy Vegan Side Dish Gluten-Free Dinner

Ingredients

  • 2 bell peppers (any color), cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 large zucchini, cut into 1/2-inch half-moons
  • 1 large yellow squash, cut into 1/2-inch half-moons
  • 1 red onion, cut into 1-inch chunks
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 medium eggplant, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 head of broccoli, cut into florets
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons dried Italian herbs (or 1 tablespoon fresh herbs like rosemary, thyme, and oregano)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • Fresh herbs for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Line two large baking sheets with parchment paper.

  2. In a large bowl, combine all the cut vegetables.

  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, minced garlic, dried herbs, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using).

  4. Pour the oil mixture over the vegetables and toss until all pieces are evenly coated.

  5. Spread the vegetables in a single layer across the two baking sheets, making sure not to overcrowd them.

  6. Roast in the preheated oven for 20 minutes, then remove from the oven and stir the vegetables.

  7. Return to the oven and roast for an additional 15 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and caramelized at the edges.

  8. Remove from the oven and drizzle with balsamic vinegar while still hot.

  9. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired and serve warm.

Notes

  • Feel free to customize this recipe with your favorite vegetables. Root vegetables like carrots, parsnips, and sweet potatoes work well too.
  • For best results, cut all vegetables into similar-sized pieces to ensure even cooking.
  • Don't overcrowd the baking sheets, as this will cause the vegetables to steam rather than roast.
  • Leftovers can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 4 days and are delicious cold in salads or reheated.
  • These roasted vegetables make an excellent side dish but can also be used in pasta, grain bowls, or as a pizza topping.
